<h2>Description</h2><div><p>In this problem, you are given a sequence <i>S</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>S</i><sub>2</sub>, ..., <i>S<sub>n</sub></i> of squares of different sizes. The sides of the squares are integer numbers. We locate the squares on the positive <i>x</i>-<i>y</i> quarter of the plane, such that their sides make 45 degrees with <i>x</i> and <i>y</i> axes, and one of their vertices are on <i>y</i>=0 line. Let <i>b<sub>i</sub></i> be the <i>x</i> coordinates of the bottom vertex of <i>S<sub>i</sub></i>. First, put <i>S<sub>1</sub></i> such that its left vertex lies on <i>x</i>=0. Then, put <i>S</i><sub>1</sub>, (<i>i</i> &gt; 1) at minimum <i>b<sub>i</sub></i> such that </p><ul><li><i>b<sub>i</sub></i> &gt; <i>b<sub>i</sub></i><sub>-1</sub> and</li><li>the interior of <i>S<sub>i</sub></i> does not have intersection with the interior of <i>S</i><sub>1</sub>...<i>S</i><sub><i>i</i>-1</sub>.</li></ul><p></p><center><img src="images/3347_1.GIF"></center><p></p><p>The goal is to find which squares are visible, either entirely or partially, when viewed from above. In the example above, the squares <i>S</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>S</i><sub>2</sub>, and <i>S</i><sub>4</sub> have this property. More formally, <i>S<sub>i</sub></i> is visible from above if it contains a point <i>p</i>, such that no square other than <i>S<sub>i</sub></i> intersect the vertical half-line drawn from <i>p</i> upwards. </p></div><h2>Input</h2><p>The input consists of multiple test cases. The input is terminated by a line containing a zero number.</p><h2>Output</h2><p>For each test case, output a single line containing the index of the visible squares in the input sequence, in ascending order, separated by blank characters.</p><pre><code class="language-input1">4
