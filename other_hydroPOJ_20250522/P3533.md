<h2>Description</h2><p>The Light Switching Game is played on a 1000 ¡Á 1000 ¡Á 1000 cube of cells with a light in each cell, as Figure.1 shows. Initially, most of the lights are off while exactly <i>N</i> lights are on. Two players take moves alternately. A move consists of switching the lights at the corners of a cuboid, i.e. (<i>x</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>1</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>2</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>1</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>2</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>1</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>1</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>2</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>1</sub>), (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>2</sub>) where 1 ¡Ü <i>x</i><sub>1 </sub>¡Ü<i> x</i><sub>2</sub> ¡Ü 1000, 1 ¡Ü<i>y</i><sub>1 </sub>¡Ü<i> y</i><sub>2</sub> ¡Ü 1000, 1 ¡Ü<i>z</i><sub>1 </sub>¡Ü<i> z</i><sub>2</sub> ¡Ü 1000 and the light at the corner (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>y</i><sub>2</sub>,<i>z</i><sub>2</sub>) must be on (and turned off after the move). Notice the cuboid is possibly degenerated to a rectangle, a line or even a single cell so that the player may also switching four, two or one besides eight lights in a move. The player loses the game when he can not take a move.</p><p></p><center><img src="images/3533_1.GIF"><br>Figure.1</center><p></p><p>You will find out whether the second player can win if both players play optimally.</p><h2>Input</h2><p>There are multiple test cases.
