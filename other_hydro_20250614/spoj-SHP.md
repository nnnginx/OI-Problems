<p>You are given an unordered array with n <strong>distinct</strong> numbers from 1 to n. You have to perform <strong>exactly</strong> k swaps and print the lexicographically largest array that can be obtained.</p>
<p>In one swap, you can choose any two <strong>distinct</strong> indices and swap the elements at those indices.</p>
<h3>Input</h3>
<p>First line consists of 2 integers n(1 &lt; n ¡Ü 100000) and k(0 ¡Ü k ¡Ü 10<sup>9</sup>).</p>
<p>Next line consists of n integers (a<sub>0</sub>,a<sub>1</sub>,....,a<sub>n-1</sub>).</p>
<h3>Output</h3>
<p>You have to print lexicographically largest array obtained.</p>
<h3>Example</h3>
<pre><strong>Input:</strong>
5 2</pre>
<pre>1 2 4 3 5

<strong>Output:</strong>
5 4 2 3 1</pre>
