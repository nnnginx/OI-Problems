<p>Mohamed Yasser (AKA <em>Abo-3obaida</em>) and Mohamed Ahmed (AKA <em>Nesr</em>) pretend to be rivals, the two clearly have a deep and understanding friendship.</p>
<p>One day they imagine that the city is a 2D plane with Cartesian coordinate system, the two rivals are located in point (0,0), and they are targeting point (X,Y).</p>
<p>They can make two moves only:<br>1) move right to the point (X + 1, Y).<br>2) move up to the point (X, Y + 1).</p>
<p><em>Nesr</em> immediately said: "I've figured out how many ways are there to reach our target".<br><em>Abo-3obaida</em> replied: "I'll not lose this challenge".</p>
<p>Could you help <em>Abo-3obaida</em> to figure out how many ways to the target?!</p>
<p>Since the required number may be very large, find its remainder of division by 1000000007 (10<sup>9</sup>&nbsp;+ 7).</p>
<h4><span style="font-size: 1.17em;"><strong>Input</strong></span></h4>
<p>The first line of input contains an integer <strong>T</strong> (1 &lt;= <strong>T</strong> &lt;= 1000) followed by T test cases.</p>
<p>Each case contains two space-separated integers &nbsp;<strong>X</strong>, &nbsp;<strong>Y</strong> (0 &lt;= <strong>X</strong>, <strong>Y</strong> &lt;= 10<sup>6</sup>).</p>
<h3>Output</h3>
<p>For each test case, print a single integer the answer to the problem modulo 1000000007 (10<sup>9</sup>&nbsp;+ 7).</p>
<h3>Example</h3>
<pre><strong>Input:</strong>
3
1 3
2 4
5 3

<strong>Output:</strong>
4
15
56
</pre>
