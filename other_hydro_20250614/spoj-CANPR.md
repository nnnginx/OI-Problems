<p>A candyseller is selling N candies.Each candy is labelled with a positive integer ai from 1 to N.</p>
<p>He is a little orthodox and considers some positive integer L as his Lucky number.Apart from that, he sells candies&nbsp;</p>
<p>only in pairs.He would sell two candies ai and aj only if the greatest number which divides both ai&nbsp;</p>
<p>and aj is his lovely number L.</p>
<p>To promote his candy bussiness, he has come up with an offer to customers.He would give some candies for</p>
<p>free to the first customer who can exactly tell the total possible number of ways W in which candy seller</p>
<p>can sell one pair of candies among the N available.The customer is required to adhere to his selling policy</p>
<p>as mentioned above.</p>
<p>We want you to grab the opportunity to win the free candies by telling the number of ways W.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Input</h3>
<p>First line of input contains a positive integer T(1&lt;=T&lt;=100000) i.e. the number of test cases to follow.</p>
<p>Then T lines follow, containing two positive integers N (1&lt;=N&lt;=1000000) and L(1&lt;=L&lt;=1000000) i.e. number of candies and lucky number respectively</p>
<p>for each test case.</p>
<h3>Output</h3>
<p>For every test case,output a single non-negative integer W.</p>
<p>Note: Pairs (x,y) and (y,x) are considered different and both will contribute to W.</p>
<h3>Example</h3>
<pre><strong>Input:</strong>
2
5 1
3 2

<strong>Output:</strong>
19
1
</pre>
<pre>Testcase 2: (2,2) is the only possible pair.</pre>
