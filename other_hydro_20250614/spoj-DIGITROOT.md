<p>A <em>Digit Root</em> is a feature of numbers used in mathematical  recreation, but can also be used to check the results of simple  operations such as sum and multiplication. One of its main properties is  that the <em>Digit Root</em> of a number is always equal to <em>Digit Root</em> of the sum of its digits.</p>
<p>For example, the <em>Digit Root</em> of 18446744073709551615 is  the same as 87 because 1+8+4+4+6+7+4+4+0+7+3+7+0+9+5+5+1+6+1+5=87, and is the  same of 15 because 8+7=15, which in turn is the same as 6, since 1+5=6.  Therefore the <em>Digit Root</em> of all these numbers is 6 since 6 is its own  <em>Digit Root</em>.</p>
<p>Your task is, given the two integers, <strong>B</strong> and <strong>E</strong>, calculate the <em>Digit Root</em> of <strong>B</strong><sup><strong>E</strong></sup> (<strong>B</strong> raised to th <strong>E</strong>-th power).</p>
</div>

<h3>Output</h3>
<p>The output consists of a single line containing the<em>Digit Root</em>.</p>
