<p>A number is called prime-lover, if sum of its digits in base-3 is a prime number. For example 2, 6, and 31 are prime-lovers, sum of digits of all these numbers are prime.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">2=(2)<sub>3</sub>&nbsp;,&nbsp; 6=(20)<sub>3</sub> , 31=(1011)<sub>3</sub></p>
<p>Checking whether a number is prime-lover or not is too easy for this contest, so solve this problem:</p>
<p>Given two integers N, K. Can you calculate K¡¯th smallest prime-lover which is not greater than N?</p>
<h3>Input</h3>
<p>The first line of input indicates the number of test cases (There will be at most 1000 test cases)</p>
<p>Each test case consists of two space-separated integers N, K. (1 ¡Ü&nbsp;N, K ¡Ü&nbsp;10<sup>13</sup>)</p>
<h3>Output</h3>
<p>For each test case, print the answer to the problem. If there is no such number, print -1.</p>
<h3>Example</h3>
<pre><strong>Input:</strong>
3
10 3
10 6
10 7

<strong>Output:</strong>
5

-1</pre>
<p>Prime-Lover Numbers not greater than 10 are:</p>
<p>2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 10</p>
