<p>60 is divisible by 2, 3, 4 and 5. No smaller number than 60 have this property.</p>

<h3>Input</h3>
<p>
On the first line, you will be given two integers
 <em>T</em> (the number of test cases), and
 <em>M</em> (an integer).
<br>
Each of the next <em>T</em> lines contains an integer <em>N</em>.
</p>

<h3>Output</h3>
<p>
For each test case, output on one line, the smallest number that is divisible
by all integers from 1 to <em>N</em> (inclusive).
<br>
As the answer may be a big number, output it modulo <em>M</em>.
</p>

<h3>Example</h3>

<pre><b>Input:</b>
1 1000000007
5
<b>Output:</b>
60
</pre>


<h3>Constraints</h3>
<pre>T &lt;= 10^5
10^8 &lt;= M &lt;= 2¡Á10^9, a prime number
0 &lt; N &lt; 10^8
</pre>
