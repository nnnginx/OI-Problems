<p style="border: 0px; font-variant-numeric: inherit; font-variant-east-asian: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: &quot;Whitney SSm A&quot;, &quot;Whitney SSm B&quot;, Avenir, &quot;Segoe UI&quot;, Ubuntu, &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin: 12px 0px 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; color: #39424e;">Given the bounderies&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">L</span>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">R</span>&nbsp;of a range, answer how many&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">k-pairs</span>&nbsp;are there? k-pair is defined as a pair of numbers&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">(p, p+k)</span>&nbsp;where&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">p</span>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">p+k</span>&nbsp;are both prime numbers.&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: 600; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">In this problem, (p, p+k) is a k-pair between the boundary of L and R if both p and p+k are prime numbers and inside the boundary of L and R (inclusive).</span>&nbsp;For example, if the L = 10 and R = 20 and k = 2 then there are two k-pairs (11, 13) and (17, 19) and if k = 4 in the same range then there is only one k-pair (13, 17). Note that, (7, 11) and (19, 23) pairs are not within the boundary of the range and thus not k-pairs in this case.</p>
